# PetCare FSR Sensor Update Design

## Decision Summary

- Use two RP2350 Pico 2 W nodes with C++ Pico SDK firmware. Both CMake profiles request `-DPICO_BOARD=pico2_w -DPICO_PLATFORM=rp2350`; the pinned SDK expands that platform alias, so their caches must report `PICO_BOARD=pico2_w` and resolved `PICO_PLATFORM=rp2350-arm-s`.
- Entrance node: one SHT31 and one LD2410C.
- Pet-zone node: one SHT31, one LD2410C, two HX711 load cells for food/water, and three FSR408 sensors for the bed.
- Keep the third purchased SHT31 as a tested spare.
- Remove BME280, PIR, reed-switch, and bed HX711 requirements from this MVP.
- Keep exactly two behavior types: `eating` and `resting`; entrance presence remains informational and creates neither behaviors nor anomalies.
- Persist exactly two non-medical warning anomaly types: `no_meal_12h` and `bed_sensor_mismatch`.
- Keep alerts dashboard-only through `anomaly_events`, `GET /api/anomalies`, and exact WebSocket messages `{type:'anomaly_alert',payload:AnomalyEventOut}`.
- Keep webcam inference on the PC and raw video local and ephemeral.
- Use the user-selected `warm-homecare` dashboard direction: a warm ivory operational canvas, deep green primary actions, restrained terracotta accents, and dense evidence-first tables without medical or marketing styling.
- This design and the matching `.omo` plan/draft supersede the proposal's legacy node count, sensor list, status extras, CRUD surface, database shapes, alerts, and video-storage examples wherever they conflict.
- Planning and all final gates use `gpt-5.6-sol max`; implementation routing remains exactly the table in `Task Model Routing`.

## Approaches Considered

1. **Backend-owned calibration and occupancy (selected):** Pico publishes three raw FSR ADC channels. FastAPI stores baselines, applies hysteresis, fuses camera detections, and serves the reset action. This keeps firmware small and avoids reverse MQTT commands or flash-write policy.
2. **Pico-owned calibration:** Pico stores baselines and publishes occupancy. Rejected because the dashboard reset button then needs a command protocol, acknowledgement, and flash persistence.
3. **Hybrid raw plus derived state:** Pico and backend both classify occupancy. Rejected because duplicated thresholds can disagree without improving the MVP.

## Hardware Topology

Both firmware profiles are C++ targets for the RP2350 Pico 2 W. Each invocation must request `PICO_BOARD=pico2_w` and the `PICO_PLATFORM=rp2350` alias, while artifact acceptance requires the CMake cache to report `PICO_BOARD=pico2_w` and the pinned SDK's resolved `PICO_PLATFORM=rp2350-arm-s`; any mismatch is the wrong target and fails before artifact acceptance.

### `entrance-01`

- SHT31 on I2C0: SDA GP4, SCL GP5, 7-bit address `0x44`.
- LD2410C: sensor `UART_TX` to Pico UART1 RX GP9 at 256000 8N1. Leave sensor `UART_RX` and Pico UART1 TX disconnected.
- Publishes temperature, humidity, moving-target presence, and stationary-target presence.

### `petzone-01`

- SHT31 on I2C0: SDA GP4, SCL GP5, 7-bit address `0x44`.
- LD2410C: sensor `UART_TX` to Pico UART1 RX GP9 at 256000 8N1. Leave sensor `UART_RX` and Pico UART1 TX disconnected.
- Food HX711: DOUT GP10 and SCK GP11. Water HX711: DOUT GP12 and SCK GP13. The pairs are independent and never share a clock or calibration.
- FSR408 left/center/right dividers: ADC GP26/GP27/GP28 respectively.
- Publishes scalar readings once per second for each FSR channel; no weight unit is assigned to FSR data.

This map is collision-free and normative for firmware, wiring documentation, host fixtures, and physical acceptance. The third SHT31 is a tested spare and is not assigned a bus address in either installed node.

The FSR installation also needs three fixed resistors, a stable 3.3 V divider, and a thin load-distribution sheet. Wire each channel `3.3 V -> FSR408 -> ADC node -> 10 kOhm -> GND`; pressure therefore normally raises the ADC count and matches the backend default polarity `+1`. Firmware configuration owns only pins, cadence, and electrical facts. Backend configuration exclusively owns per-channel polarity/stability and aggregate entry/exit thresholds because mounting still requires calibration.

The electrical contract is exact:

- Power every SHT31 and HX711 logic rail from 3.3 V, use only 3.3 V I2C pull-ups, and make all grounds common.
- Power each FSR divider from 3.3 V through its 10 kOhm starter resistor.
- Power each LD2410C VCC from a regulated 5 V supply rated for at least 200 mA. Only the module's documented 3.3 V `UART_TX` connects to Pico UART1 RX GP9 at 256000 8N1. Leave LD2410C `UART_RX` and Pico UART1 TX disconnected.
- Every Pico GPIO/ADC input must remain in `0..3.3 V`; never feed 5 V into a Pico pin.

Food owns `FOOD_TARE_RAW` and `FOOD_COUNTS_PER_GRAM`; water owns `WATER_TARE_RAW` and `WATER_COUNTS_PER_GRAM`. Each `counts_per_gram` must be finite and greater than zero. Calibrated grams are `(raw - tare_raw) / counts_per_gram`; values and ownership never cross bowls. The per-bowl fixture is exact: `(900 raw - 100 tare_raw) / 10 counts_per_gram = 80 g` for food and independently for water.

### Cadence and sampling order

| Output | Cadence | Sampling rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `temperature`, `humidity` | every 30 seconds | one SHT31 transaction, then emit temperature before humidity |
| `presence_moving`, `presence_stationary` | every 1 second | one parsed LD2410C frame, then emit moving before stationary |
| `food_weight`, `water_weight` | every 1 second on `petzone-01` | sample food HX711 before water HX711 with their own tare/scale |
| `bed_pressure_left`, `bed_pressure_center`, `bed_pressure_right` | every 1 second on `petzone-01` | sample left, center, then right; publish raw ADC only |
| device status | immediately after MQTT connect and every 10 seconds | emit after every sensor output due at that monotonic timestamp |

At a shared due timestamp, a profile emits only its supported outputs in this global order: `temperature`, `humidity`, `presence_moving`, `presence_stationary`, `food_weight`, `water_weight`, `bed_pressure_left`, `bed_pressure_center`, `bed_pressure_right`, status. The scheduler drains all due outputs with `while (next_due(now_ms, out))`; invalid reads suppress only their own output and never become zero. Firmware never interprets FSR polarity, baseline, stability, occupancy, fusion, or rest.

## MQTT Contracts

Sensor topics are exactly `home/pico/{device_id}/sensor/{sensor_type}`. The payload map is exact:

| Sensor type | `unit` | `value` |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `temperature` | `C` | finite JSON number |
| `humidity` | `%` | finite JSON number |
| `presence_moving`, `presence_stationary` | `bool` | JSON boolean |
| `food_weight`, `water_weight` | `g` | finite JSON number |
| `bed_pressure_left`, `bed_pressure_center`, `bed_pressure_right` | `adc` | JSON integer from 0 through 4095 |

The production firmware no longer emits `bed_weight`. Existing host fixtures and tests are updated rather than carrying a disabled legacy field.

Every sensor payload owns fixed 64-byte topic and 256-byte payload buffers and serializes exactly the ordered keys `device_id,sensor_type,value,unit,observed_at`. Sensor publications use QoS 1 and `retain=false`.

Status uses exactly `home/pico/{device_id}/status` and the ordered three-field `DeviceStatusIn` payload `device_id,status,observed_at`; `status` is only `online|offline`. It uses the same fixed buffers and zero-allocation serializer as sensor telemetry. An online status is published immediately after each MQTT connection and every 10 seconds at QoS 1 with `retain=true`. A graceful disconnect first publishes a current offline status at QoS 1 with `retain=true`. Each connection also registers a QoS-1 retained offline LWT serialized at connection time; its `observed_at` is the synchronized connection instant and therefore marks when that will was armed, while broker receipt marks the actual unclean disconnect. An accepted offline/LWT envelope makes RuleWorker set status offline when that FIFO item is applied and never decreases `last_seen_at`.

This exact status shape supersedes the proposal example containing `firmware_version`, `ip`, `uptime_sec`, and `timestamp`; strict ingestion rejects those legacy keys. Before intake, each sensor stream watermark is loaded as persisted `MAX(observed_at)` or empty. `RuleIngress.begin('mqtt')` atomically registers backend receipt UTC/monotonic time before validation. Any payload more than 5 seconds in the future is rejected with no row, device, or timer mutation and resolves its ticket to a tombstone. A unique sensor reading at or after that stream watermark is persisted, advances the ingestion watermark, and resolves one committed envelope; only RuleWorker then sets `last_seen_at=max(previous,observed_at)`, marks the device online, and refreshes its receipt deadline. A unique older sensor reading is persisted for audit with result `stored_out_of_order`, resolves a tombstone, and does not advance the watermark, device state, `last_seen_at`, or receipt deadline. The database unique key makes an exact sensor duplicate a tombstone no-op. An online status is live only when its payload age at receipt is in `[-5 seconds,30 seconds]`; an older retained online status is ignored with result `stale_online` and a tombstone. Offline/LWT is accepted regardless of age or backward ordering once it passes schema/future validation and resolves one status envelope; only RuleWorker sets offline, never decreases `last_seen_at`, and does not refresh the online receipt deadline. Status tuples deduplicate only within the active process; after restart, a retained offline tuple is deliberately accepted again as current broker state. RuleWorker synthesizes offline after 30 seconds without an applied live sensor/online-status envelope; staleness uses the shared injected monotonic clock.

Wi-Fi and MQTT reconnect delays are exactly 1, 2, 4, 8, 16, then 30 seconds, capped at 30 until success and reset to 1 after success. Publications are not retained in an unbounded firmware backlog; a failed read or disconnected interval produces no later timestamped replay.

### UTC clock contract

- On every Wi-Fi connection, firmware starts lwIP SNTP with primary `pool.ntp.org` and fallback `time.cloudflare.com`. It retries every 15 seconds until valid and resynchronizes every 6 hours.
- UTC is valid only when Unix time is at least `1704067200` (`2024-01-01T00:00:00Z`). Firmware does not connect MQTT or serialize any sensor/status payload before a valid sync.
- A successful sync records one `(utc_ms, monotonic_ms)` anchor. Every due batch derives `observed_at` from that anchor plus monotonic elapsed time and formats `YYYY-MM-DDTHH:MM:SS.sssZ`; all outputs in one due batch share that derived instant.
- A forward resync replaces the anchor. A resync candidate earlier than the last published UTC instant is a backward jump: invalidate the wall-clock anchor, publish nothing, discard due outputs rather than buffering them, and retry SNTP until a candidate is at least one millisecond later than the last published instant. No naive, pre-threshold, duplicate-backward, or otherwise invalid timestamp is published.
- On reboot there is no trusted persisted wall clock; SNTP synchronization is required again before MQTT. The prebuilt LWT remains the only broker-generated status during an unclean loss.

## Bed Calibration

The dashboard exposes one `침대 영점 재설정` command. `POST /api/bed/calibration` enqueues a `CalibrateBedCommand` and evaluates the exact window `(now - 60 seconds, now]` for all three pressure channels. It succeeds only when:

- every channel has at least 45 valid samples;
- no selected whole-frame `dog_001` or `cat_001` normalized bounding box has positive-area half-open intersection with the enabled bed ROI during the sample window. For box `[bbox_x,bbox_x+bbox_width) x [bbox_y,bbox_y+bbox_height)` and zone `[x1,x2) x [y1,y2)`, overlap is exactly `bbox_x < x2 AND x1 < bbox_x+bbox_width AND bbox_y < y2 AND y1 < bbox_y+bbox_height`; edge touching is not overlap;
- the camera was available for the full open-start, closed-end interval according to the `CameraService` one-second availability ring buffer;
- each channel's exact stability value `max(raw) - min(raw)` is at or below its configured limit.

Backend environment knobs are optional and use these defaults: `FSR_POLARITY_LEFT|CENTER|RIGHT=+1`; `FSR_STABILITY_COUNTS_LEFT|CENTER|RIGHT=40`; `FSR_ENTRY_THRESHOLD=450`; `FSR_EXIT_THRESHOLD=250`. Polarities validate as exactly `-1|+1`; each stability limit validates in `0..4095`; aggregate thresholds validate `0 <= exit < entry <= 12285`. Any invalid value fails application startup. These knobs remain because real hardware requires tuning; firmware never owns or copies them.

Calibration evaluates failures once in this strict precedence: `sensor_unavailable`, `insufficient_samples`, `camera_unavailable`, `occupied`, `unstable`. `sensor_unavailable` applies when a channel has no valid sample or its newest valid sample is older than 3 seconds at `now`; `insufficient_samples` applies next when a remaining channel has fewer than 45 samples. `unstable` compares the exact range formula above. The `channels` array is deterministic `left,center,right` filtered to failures for those three channel-specific codes; it is empty for `camera_unavailable` and `occupied`. Only the first code is returned.

The backend stores all three median baselines, sample counts, polarities, stability limits, aggregate thresholds, and the exact window atomically. Any 409 or transaction failure preserves the previous valid calibration in full.

For each channel, the backend computes `max(0, polarity * (raw - baseline))`. The three deltas are summed. Entry and exit thresholds are separate configuration values:

- occupied candidate: aggregate delta at or above the entry threshold for 2 seconds;
- empty candidate: aggregate delta at or below the exit threshold for 7 seconds.

No automatic baseline drift correction is included in the MVP. Recalibration is required after moving or washing the cushion.

## Eating Rule

`eating` is one deterministic food-bowl session rule, not a frame-level label:

1. A `dog_001` or `cat_001` center must remain continuously inside enabled `food_bowl` for at least 30.000 seconds. Camera frames and food readings are fresh when `0 <= evaluation_time - observed_at <= 3.000 seconds`; an unavailable interval resets, rather than pauses, pre-confirmation dwell.
2. At ROI entry, capture `pre_entry_weight_g` as the median of at least five valid food-weight samples in `(entry_time - 10 seconds, entry_time]`, with the newest sample fresh. Current weight is the median of at least four valid samples in `(evaluation_time - 5 seconds, evaluation_time]`.
3. Confirm once when dwell is at least 30 seconds and `pre_entry_weight_g - current_weight_g >= 5.0 g`, no later than 120 seconds after entry. Equality at 30 seconds and 5.0 g passes; smaller values fail. If confirmation has not occurred by 120 seconds, disarm that entry until rearm.
4. Maintain independent stable subject dwell, but one bowl lock. If both subjects qualify for one weight drop, choose the earlier dwell start; an exact tie chooses `dog_001`. Insert one `BehaviorEvent` with `started_at=dwell_start` and the confirming camera/sensor IDs. The database source-key/unique constraints and processed event IDs make callback retries idempotent.
5. After confirmation, close when the chosen subject has been continuously outside `food_bowl` for 5 seconds; persist `ended_at` as the last inside-frame instant. A camera or food-sensor loss longer than 3 seconds closes at the last jointly fresh instant. `duration_seconds` is nonnegative elapsed seconds rounded half up to the nearest integer.
6. Rearm only after the bowl has no qualifying pet for 10 continuous seconds and that interval contains at least five valid food-weight samples. A subject that remains in the ROI cannot create a second event from the same decrease.
7. At process restart, close an orphan open eating row at the latest persisted jointly qualifying camera/sensor instant, never later than startup; pre-confirmation dwell is not resumed. The first post-start event begins new dwell, and uniqueness prevents a duplicate completed session.

Tests cover `29.999/30.000` seconds, `4.999/5.000 g`, freshness equality/expiry, the 120-second limit, both subject/tie paths, callback duplication, close/rearm boundaries, sensor/camera loss, and restart recovery. Local-live drives the real 30-second dwell and 5 g decrease through MQTT plus file/mock camera input.

## Camera Fusion And Rest Sessions

Every USB, file, and mock source adapter must emit an exact NumPy `uint8` BGR frame with shape `(480,640,3)`. The USB adapter requests 640x480 and verifies every returned frame; file and mock adapters apply the same verification. Any other dtype or shape is rejected before inference, annotation, persistence, or MJPEG publication, marks camera status offline with reason `invalid_frame_shape`, and performs no resize, letterbox, crop, or coordinate transform. Inference, annotation, MJPEG, detection geometry, and ROI editing therefore share the same verified 640x480 coordinate space.

The MVP supports one subject per pet class: `dog_001` and `cat_001`. Ultralytics `xyxy` coordinates are float32 and are normalized before ranking or persistence. For each allowed-class candidate require finite confidence/coordinates, `0<=confidence<=1`, and raw `x1<x2`,`y1<y2`. Clamp x coordinates to `[0,640]` and y coordinates to `[0,480]`; after clamping compute integer `left=floor(x1)`, `top=floor(y1)`, `right=ceil(x2)`, and `bottom=ceil(y2)`. Reject `right<=left` or `bottom<=top`. Persist `bbox_x=left`, `bbox_y=top`, `bbox_width=right-left`, `bbox_height=bottom-top`, `center_x=floor((left+right)/2)`, and `center_y=floor((top+bottom)/2)`. Normalize/reject every candidate first, then for each frame retain only one highest-confidence normalized detection per class over the whole frame. Equal-confidence same-class candidates choose the lexicographically smallest `(bbox_x,bbox_y,bbox_width,bbox_height)` tuple; candidates equal on that normalized tuple are semantically identical and collapse to one fact.

Only after that whole-frame per-class selection does zone membership use the center point and the exact half-open predicate `x1 <= center_x < x2 AND y1 <= center_y < y2`; `zone_for_center`, food-bowl dwell, and in-bed facts all use this predicate, so a center on a shared right/bottom edge belongs only to the adjacent zone. Thus a higher-confidence same-class detection outside the bed suppresses a lower-confidence same-class detection inside it for that frame. The immutable frame fact stores `bed_subject_ids` in fixed `dog_001,cat_001` order filtered to selected subjects whose centers are in bed, plus `selected_bed_subject_id`. Selection is the sole in-bed subject when only one is present; when both are present, the higher confidence wins and an exact confidence tie selects `dog_001`. Person detections remain informational, and multiple same-class pet identity tracking or sequential track identity is explicitly excluded. Calibration uses the positive-area bounding-box intersection predicate defined in `Bed Calibration` on the same selected normalized class detections, rather than center membership.

Pressure classification is exact. All three channels must be calibrated and no more than 3 seconds stale. A successful empty-bed calibration sets `pressure_state=empty` immediately. On process start, a loaded calibration begins at `pressure_state=unavailable` until the first fresh complete three-channel aggregate; that first aggregate selects `occupied` at `>= entry`, `empty` at `<= exit`, and deterministically seeds `empty` when it is strictly between the thresholds. Later mid-band values retain the previous `empty|occupied` state and reset both candidate timers. Aggregate delta `sum(max(0, polarity * (raw - baseline)))` starts an occupied candidate at `>= entry` and confirms after 2 continuous seconds; any lower sample resets that candidate. It starts an empty candidate at `<= exit` and confirms after 7 continuous seconds; any higher sample resets that candidate. Unavailable/uncalibrated input yields that explicit state and resets both timers. Candidate time never pauses.

A camera fact is fresh only when the latest processed frame is no more than 3 seconds old. The complete fusion/side-effect table is:

| Pressure | Camera | Fusion | Rest action | Mismatch action |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| unavailable or uncalibrated | any | `unavailable` | close open rest as `sensor_loss` at the last complete-pressure/confirmed instant | reset pending timer |
| ready empty/occupied | unavailable or stale | `unavailable` | close open rest as `camera_loss` at the last fresh confirmed-camera instant | reset pending timer |
| empty | fresh no pet | `empty` | confirm pressure exit; close as `pressure_exit` at the empty-candidate start | clear pending timer |
| empty | fresh dog and/or cat in bed | `sensor_check` | close any open rest as `pressure_exit` at the empty-candidate start | start/continue `sensor_check` for `selected_bed_subject_id` |
| occupied | fresh dog and/or cat in bed | `confirmed_rest` | apply the deterministic owner/candidate rules below | clear pending timer |
| occupied | fresh no pet | `unconfirmed_pressure` | apply the owner-absence rule below | start/continue subjectless `unconfirmed_pressure` immediately |

`BedStatus.camera_confirmed` is a state-confirmation flag, not a raw visibility flag: it is `true` exactly when `fusion_state=confirmed_rest` and `false` for `unavailable|empty|unconfirmed_pressure|sensor_check`. Calibration success, restart bootstrap, the first mid-band sample, and every fusion row are tested against this truth table.

With occupied pressure and no open rest owner, `selected_bed_subject_id` becomes a candidate. That exact subject must remain selected continuously with occupied pressure for 2.000 seconds before the rest opens; a selection flip, missing selection, stale/unavailable camera, or non-occupied pressure resets the candidate timer. An open owner is retained while that owner remains in `bed_subject_ids`, even when the other pet has higher confidence or is selected. Owner absence starts a 3.000-second camera-exit timer at the first fresh frame without the owner. If the owner returns before the boundary, the absence and replacement candidate reset. If absence reaches 3.000 seconds, close the old rest with `ended_at` equal to the absence start. A replacement may be tracked during that interval and opens after the close once its own uninterrupted 2.000-second occupied-pressure confirmation is satisfied; its `started_at` is its confirmation instant. This is the only handoff path.

Entering a mismatch sets `(kind,subject_identity,started_at)`, where `unconfirmed_pressure` uses subject identity `petzone-01` and `sensor_check` uses the selected dog/cat. The same tuple advances continuously; exactly at 30 seconds it attempts one warning for that episode. A kind or selected-subject change resets `started_at` to the transition instant. `confirmed_rest` or `empty` clears the episode. Any unavailable, stale, or uncalibrated interval also resets it; timers never pause. A dedupe rejection within 15 minutes marks that episode handled, so it cannot emit later merely because the dedupe window expires; a cleared/reset episode must rearm and remain continuous for a new 30 seconds.

`RestCloseReason` is exactly `pressure_exit|camera_exit|sensor_loss|camera_loss|shutdown|restart`. Confirming a candidate atomically inserts one open `resting` behavior row and one linked open `rest_session`, both with `started_at=confirmation_at`. Closing atomically updates both rows with the same `ended_at`, half-up rounded nonnegative `duration_seconds`, and the rest close reason. `pressure_exit` and `camera_exit` are persisted only after their 7-second/3-second confirmation, but use the candidate-start timestamp as `ended_at`. Sensor/camera loss uses the last jointly confirmed instant. Controlled shutdown closes at shutdown time only while still confirmed, otherwise at `last_confirmed_at`. Startup closes every orphan open pair at persisted `last_confirmed_at` with `restart` before fresh signals can open another; every candidate, dwell, absence, and mismatch timer starts empty. Global partial unique indexes permit only one open `resting` behavior and one open `rest_session` for the physical bed.

- `bed_sensor_mismatch` uses the transition behavior above: `unconfirmed_pressure` means occupied pressure with no fresh dog or cat in `pet_bed`; it has `subject_id=null` and source/dedupe identity `petzone-01`. `sensor_check` means a fresh selected dog/cat in `pet_bed` with empty pressure and uses that selected subject. Persist the mismatch kind and deduplicate for 15 minutes per identity/anomaly type.
- `no_meal_12h` is ineligible while the subject has an open eating row. Otherwise its source is the latest closed eating row, `eligible_at=ended_at+12 hours`, `occurred_at=eligible_at`, and source key `{subject}:no_meal_12h:{eligible_at UTC}`. A later eating row replaces that eligibility; deduplicate for 15 minutes per subject and anomaly type.
- Persist an anomaly before broadcasting `{type:'anomaly_alert',payload:AnomalyEventOut}`. Both anomaly types have severity `warning`; neither makes a danger, diagnosis, or medical claim.

Behavior evidence is deterministic. Eating uses the selected subject's highest-confidence in-bowl camera event from the confirming frame and the newest food reading participating in the confirming current median, breaking an exact timestamp tie by larger row ID. Rest uses the candidate subject's in-bed camera event from the frame that reaches 2.000 seconds and the newest of the three pressure readings in that confirming aggregate, breaking equal timestamps by channel order `left,center,right`. These IDs form the existing behavior source key.

The dashboard reports current rest duration, today's confirmed rest total, nighttime bed-exit count, seven-day average comparison when enough history exists, camera confirmation, raw/baseline/delta values for all three channels, and a newest-first warning list with mismatch-kind copy. UI copy says `휴식 추정`, `야간 침대 이탈`, `12시간 식사 기록 없음`, and `침대 센서 확인 필요`, not danger or medical sleep diagnosis.

## Local-Day Rest Metrics

- Store every reading and session timestamp in UTC. Convert reporting boundaries with `zoneinfo.ZoneInfo("Asia/Seoul")`; never approximate a local day with a fixed UTC offset or a rolling 24-hour window.
- Let `D` be the current `Asia/Seoul` calendar date. Today's interval is `[D local midnight, now]`. The baseline intervals are exactly the seven complete local calendar days `D-7` through `D-1`, with every local boundary converted to UTC before querying.
- History is sufficient only when the earliest valid `petzone-01` pressure reading is at or before `D-7` local midnight. Before that point, `complete_days` is the count from 0 through 6 of baseline days whose local start is at or after that earliest reading; `status='insufficient_data'` and `baseline_seconds`, `difference_seconds`, and `percent_change` are null.
- Once history is sufficient, each baseline day contributes a total, including zero when it has no `rest_session`. Clip every closed session to each interval. Clip an open current session to `now` only for today's interval.
- `baseline_seconds` is the integer mean of the seven daily totals rounded half up. If it is zero, return `status='zero_baseline'`, `difference_seconds=today_seconds`, and `percent_change=null`. Otherwise return `status='ready'`, `difference_seconds=today_seconds-baseline_seconds`, and `percent_change=100*difference_seconds/baseline_seconds` rounded half up to one decimal. Both sufficient states have `complete_days=7`.
- Count a nighttime bed exit only for a session ending with `close_reason='pressure_exit'` whose `Asia/Seoul` local end date is `D` and whose local end time is at or after 22:00 or before 06:00.

## Backend Concurrency And API Boundary

- `DomainEvent` is a closed immutable union: `SensorReadingCommitted(reading_id,device_id,sensor_type,observed_at)`, `DeviceStatusCommitted(device_id,status,observed_at)`, and `CameraFrameCommitted(camera_id,observed_at,detection_ids:tuple[int,...],bed_subject_ids:tuple[dog_001|cat_001,...],selected_bed_subject_id:dog_001|cat_001|null)`. The subject tuple has the fixed order above and the selected value follows the exact confidence/tie rule. No variant carries a SQLAlchemy session, mutable collection, frame bytes, credential, or uncommitted row.
- The serial Paho pipeline and serial camera worker call `RuleIngress.begin(source)` as their first application action, before validation or persistence. Under one standard-library `Condition`, `RuleIngress` reads the single injected `RuleClock`, captures paired `(received_at_utc,received_at_monotonic)`, allocates a strictly increasing `ticket_id`, and registers exactly one outstanding ticket per producer. That locked registration is the receipt-order linearization point; producers never supply or rewrite receipt time.
- After registration, each producer opens a fresh session and either resolves its ticket to one immutable committed `RuleEnvelope(ticket_id,event,received_at_utc,received_at_monotonic)` after commit/close, or resolves it to an explicit tombstone on validation rejection, duplicate/stale disposition, rollback, or graceful cancellation. `RuleIngress` keeps a ticket-ordered deque, so reverse producer completion cannot release a later ticket first and an unresolved earlier ticket cannot be skipped. Behavior/rest/anomaly mutations also commit before dashboard enqueue/broadcast.
- `RuleIngress` has an exact ready capacity of 1024 committed events plus at most one retained committed-but-not-admitted ticket for each of the two serial producers. A producer whose committed ticket cannot enter ready capacity sets health `queue=full`, retains and retries that same ticket every 250 ms, and accepts no next item; admission occurs only in ticket order. Rejections and database failures resolve tombstones in `finally` and therefore cannot block the watermark. There is no silent drop or reorder, and health returns to `ok` only after the retained ticket is admitted and ready depth falls below capacity.
- `CalibrateBedCommand` is immutable but is not a `DomainEvent`. `RuleIngress.try_submit_command` gets one atomic 250 ms admission attempt; failure creates no ticket, completes no Future, changes no baseline, and returns HTTP 503 `queue_unavailable`. It is not retried behind the client's back.
- Exactly one `RuleWorker` consumes admitted envelopes, commands, and tombstones in ticket order. It alone owns mutable device, eating, bed, anomaly, and session state and opens a fresh session per item. Processed committed IDs/source keys make a retained-ticket retry harmless. The public/persisted `DomainEvent` union remains unchanged.
- `RuleIngress` and `RuleWorker` share the one injected `RuleClock`; only `RuleWorker` owns every device-offline, input-stale, dwell, pressure, owner-absence, mismatch, and no-meal deadline and every synthesized timeout transition. At a due boundary it seals a deadline barrier while holding the same `Condition` used by `begin`: every ticket already registered with receipt time at or before the boundary must resolve and be released first, while a registration linearized after the sealed barrier is later even if a coarse test clock still reports equality. A qualifying receipt registered exactly at the boundary before the barrier therefore wins deterministically.
- Before an envelope at monotonic time `t`, `RuleWorker` fires sealed deadlines strictly earlier than `t`; it applies the envelope next, then fires still-valid deadlines at `t`. With no eligible envelope, it seals and fires due deadlines by `(due_monotonic,RuleWorker-owned insertion order)` only after the ingress watermark proves no unresolved or retained pre-boundary ticket exists. Scheduling stores both `due_monotonic` and paired `effective_at_utc`; persistence uses that scheduled UTC, so validation, commit, capacity, or worker delay never changes the transition timestamp. Volatile deadlines reset on restart. Only `no_meal_12h` recomputes absolute UTC eligibility from the latest persisted closed eating row and the injected UTC clock; this is source-key deduplicated and is not replay of an unqueued observation.
- Tests pause a ticket before commit, reverse MQTT/camera completion, hold ready capacity full, and exercise rejection, database rollback, graceful cancellation, shutdown, and receipt times just before, exactly at, and just after every deadline. A deadline must remain blocked only by a registered pre-boundary ticket; its tombstone or committed release unblocks the watermark, and no post-boundary ticket may rewind a sealed deadline.
- The calibration API submits its command through `RuleIngress` and awaits its `concurrent.futures.Future` through FastAPI's AnyIO-backed `run_in_threadpool`; application code does not capture an asyncio loop.
- `CameraStatus` has exactly `state: online|offline`, `fps`, `inference_ms`, `last_frame_at: UTC|null`, and `reason: string|null`. `CameraService.available_for(start_exclusive, end_inclusive) -> bool` reads its one-second availability ring, and `CameraService.shutdown() -> None` stops and joins its worker.
- `DashboardHub` uses one bounded standard-library queue and FastAPI's AnyIO-backed threadpool bridge; one async broadcaster owns sends. A full dashboard queue marks health degraded; summary/bed updates coalesce by type to the newest committed value, while an `anomaly_alert` is retried until queued or shutdown because its row is already queryable. No broadcast failure rolls back or duplicates a database row.
- Graceful shutdown is exact: stop new MQTT/camera/API intake; each producer resolves its one registered ticket in `finally` to a tombstone or finishes admitting its one committed retained envelope while `RuleWorker` drains; join producers; atomically seal one stop marker after their final ticket; let `RuleWorker` drain through that marker and join it. No registered pre-stop ticket or committed event is silently lost on this path.
- Hard crash or forced termination has no rule replay guarantee. Persisted sensor, status, camera, behavior, rest, calibration, and anomaly rows remain queryable, but rule derivations are not retroactively reconstructed from unqueued observations or empty camera frames. Before accepting fresh intake, startup closes orphan open eating and rest rows under their exact restart rules, loads the latest calibration and persisted dedupe facts, reads the latest closed eating row only to recompute the absolute `no_meal_12h` eligibility, and resets every volatile eating dwell, pressure candidate, rest-owner candidate, owner-absence, and mismatch timer. There is no outbox, processing checkpoint, frame ledger, or claim that the commit/enqueue crash window is replayed.

## Failure Handling

- Stale or missing FSR channels produce `sensor unavailable`, never zero pressure.
- MQTT topic/payload mismatch is rejected before persistence.
- Camera outage leaves raw pressure visible but makes fusion unavailable and closes confirmed rest with `camera_loss`; unavailable intervals reset every continuous dwell, pressure candidate, camera-exit, and mismatch timer rather than pausing it.
- A mismatch kind change restarts its 30-second timer. `confirmed_rest`/`empty` clears it; unavailable/stale/uncalibrated input resets it. Both mismatch kinds follow the same one-attempt-per-episode and 15-minute dedupe disposition.
- Anomaly rows commit before WebSocket broadcast. A WebSocket failure leaves the warning available from `GET /api/anomalies` and cannot create a duplicate row.
- Queue-full behavior is the producer-specific backpressure/503 contract above. Graceful shutdown drains each producer's one retained committed event; a hard crash leaves committed rows queryable but does not promise rule replay.
- Database or WebSocket failure cannot prevent MQTT/camera/RuleWorker shutdown and join.
- A failed calibration preserves the last valid baseline.
- Physical sensor and webcam checks remain `NOT RUN` until hardware is connected.
